Cold Days, Warm Meals

Hypothesis: On colder days, I eat more total calories.

This scatterplot compares my average daily temperature (°C) on the X-axis and my total calorie intake (kcal) on the Y-axis. Each circle represents one day in my dataset. Besides these two main factors, I also show how many meals I ate and how strongly food affected my emotions. Bubble size shows meals per day: smaller bubbles represent days when I ate 2 meals, and larger bubbles show days when I ate 3 meals. Bubble color shows my emotional impact after eating on a 1-5 scale. These extra elements help me see not just whether I eat more on cold days, but also how I spread my meals and how “comforting” the food feels.

Looking at the visualization, many of the colder days on the left side of the chart sit higher on the Y-axis, which means my total calories are often higher when the average temperature is low. However, the pattern is not perfect: there are a few warmer days with high calorie intake and a few colder days where I still ate less. The larger bubbles (3 meals) appear slightly more often on cold days, and several darker circles (higher emotional impact) also appear on cold, high-calorie days. From this dataset, I would say that colder weather tends to push my calories up and increase emotional or comfort eating, but other factors like mood, schedule, and cravings also play a role.
