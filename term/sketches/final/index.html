Three Visualization Proposals

Name: Jingjing Fang

About My Dataset

I recorded my daily life for 30 days, focusing on how weather and temperature affect my eating habits and emotional state.

The dataset includes the following fields:

  • High Temp (°C)
  • Low Temp (°C)
  • Weather Type
  • Total Calorie Intake (kcal)
  • Meals per Day
  • % of Hot Meals
  • Emotional Impact (0-5)

Proposal 1 - Bubble Plot

Hypothesis: As the daily temperature decreases, my total calorie intake increases. Colder weather also makes me prefer hot meals, and my mood tends to drop slightly on those days.

Data Fields: Low Temp (°C), Total Calorie Intake (kcal), % of Hot Meals, Emotional Impact (0-5)

Visual Encoding: X = Low Temp (°C), Y = Total Calories (kcal), Bubble Size = % of Hot Meals, Bubble Color = Emotional Impact (blue = low, yellow = high)

Goal: To explore how colder temperatures influence both eating habits and emotional changes through a simple but clear scatter-based visualization.

Proposal 2 - Weather Mood Calendar

Hypothesis: My emotional intensity changes with different weather types — I usually feel calmer on sunny days and lower on rainy ones. On days with lower mood, I tend to eat more meals, which appears as a larger color area inside each calendar square.

Data Fields: Date, Weather, Emotional Impact (0-5), Meals per Day

Visual Encoding: X = Date (calendar layout), Y = Weather Type, Color Hue = Weather, Color Opacity = Emotion (higher opacity = stronger emotion), Color Fill Area = Meals per Day (larger area = more meals)

Goal: To visualize daily mood and meal patterns through color transparency and area size in a compact, diary-like calendar format.

Proposal 3 - Mood Orbit

Hypothesis: Days with moderate temperature tend to cluster toward higher mood and balanced calorie intake, forming a calm outer orbit. As temperature becomes extreme — either very cold or very hot — the pattern moves inward, where mood drops and calorie intake rises.

Data Fields: Weather, High Temp (°C) / Low Temp (°C), Emotional Impact (0-5), Total Calorie Intake (kcal), % of Hot Meals

Visual Encoding: The center of the chart represents four main weather types — sunny, cloudy, rainy, and snowy. Circular orbits around the center represent temperature zones: the outer rings show warmer days, and the inner rings show colder days. Each point on an orbit corresponds to one day. The color of each point encodes mood (blue = low, yellow = high), and its distance from the center represents total calorie intake (further = more calories). The opacity of each point reflects the percentage of hot meals (higher opacity = higher proportion of hot meals).

Goal: To express the relationship between weather, temperature, emotion, and calorie intake through an orbital metaphor. The arrangement allows viewers to easily distinguish between balanced “healthy days” in the outer orbits and stressful, irregular days closer to the center.

Credits / References

Dear Data inspired me to use symbolic and emotional visual metaphors. It made me realize that weather and mood are not only data but also personal experiences that can be shown through color and rhythm.

Data-to-Viz helped me choose the right chart forms and understand which structures are better for comparing multiple variables, especially when combining categorical (weather) and numerical (temperature, calories) data.

Observable D3 Gallery gave me ideas about layout and spacing. Although I did not use any code, its circular and calendar-based examples helped me plan the overall organization of my designs.
