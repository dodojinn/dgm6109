Temperature and Daily Calorie Intake

A bubble scatterplot of my daily temperature, food intake, and emotions.

Question

This project asks a simple question: Does daily temperature affect how much I eat? I wanted to see if colder or warmer days change my total calorie intake and how I feel.

Hypothesis

As temperature decreases, the total amount of food I consume increases.

Data Overview

I collected data for the last month of the term. For each day, I recorded the high and low temperature (in °C), total calories I ate that day (in kcal), how many meals I had, the percentage of hot meals, my emotional impact from 1 (very low) to 5 (very strong), and a short weather note.

In the bubble scatterplot, the x-axis shows the average temperature for the day, the y-axis shows my total calories, bubble size shows how many meals I ate, and bubble color shows my emotional impact from light to dark red.

I collected one row of data per day for 28 days. This gave me enough points to see general trends even though it is still a small personal dataset.

Summarize

My calorie intake stays in a similar range, but there is a small tendency for slightly cooler days to have higher calories. Emotional impact does not follow a simple pattern: strong emotions appear on both lower and mid temperatures.

Visually, most bubbles fall between roughly 1200 and 1800 kcal, and there is no strong upward or downward trend across the full temperature range. Darker bubbles, representing stronger emotional impact, are scattered throughout rather than forming a specific cluster.

Based on what the visualization shows, my first idea that “cold days always make me eat much more” is only partly true. Other factors such as schedule, stress, or mood likely also influence how much I eat.
